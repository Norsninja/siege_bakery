# Plan 17 — THE LORE OF RECORD: after the wars, the giants are hungry

**Status: RECORDED 2026-07-10 from the visionary's words (the
pre-friend-test discussion session). The kernel is older — artillery
plans/02's premise, 2026-07-02: "The war is over. The catapults
remain. The giants are hungry," and the Patron named as "the
giant/dragon/refugee-boss." This document is the full articulation,
canon for this repo. It binds VOICE and TONE; it binds no mechanics.**

## The lore

Every fantasy story we've all heard ends at the victory: the rings are
destroyed, the campaign concludes, peace comes to the realm. This game
lives in the era those stories end BEFORE — the reconciliation time.
The armies disband. The giants, displaced by the great struggles, must
make the long journey home. And on the way, they need to eat.

The dwarves have committed to feeding them. The siege engines that
survived the wars were not melted down — they were turned to the only
work in the realm big enough to need them: baking colossal desserts
for enormous guests. The catapult that once threw stone now lobs
cherries. That is the whole game.

## Tone rulings (visionary, 2026-07-10 — final)

- **NOT a metaphor.** This is not commentary on real-world wars,
  refugees, or atrocities. It is a riff on the endings of the fantasy
  stories everyone has heard over and over — "what happens when the
  battles are not going on."
- **NOT bittersweet.** A fun party game. The after-war setting exists
  because the visionary's imagination asked "how do we serve giants,
  and why?" and this is the answer that makes sense — hospitality
  comedy, not elegy.
- The standing tone guard (plans/02, plans/16 §1) is this lore's
  child: nobody dies, REFUSED is huffy not hostile (a war-weary giant
  turning away a botched dessert is sad-funny, never violent), HUNGRY
  is mournful rumbling, the Vegas energy celebrates the crew.

## The economy's framing (visionary, 2026-07-10)

**THE REALM PAYS for fulfilled orders.** The giants are guests — they
never reach for a coin purse. The bakery's purse, the pay lines, and
the shop are relief contracts settled by the realm. (room.test.ts
already jokes "the realm pays its hero" — the vocabulary wanted to
exist.)

**The patrons are STILL PICKY.** Being fed for free does not dull a
giant's palate. The two-gate Judgment (order met? is it GOOD?) is
characterization — the snob, the glutton, the fickle one — not
commerce. Pickiness is where the comedy lives.

## What the lore retroactively explains (recorded so nobody re-derives)

- Why the artillery muscle memory inverts: every gamer knows how to
  lob a grenade; here the familiar skill feeds instead of destroys.
  The genre inversion (plans/02: addition, not subtraction) IS the
  lore's mechanical face.
- Why the patron LOOMS but never threatens: he is a guest at a table,
  not a boss at a gate.
- Why the line of patrons (plans/16 slice 3) reads as a queue of the
  hungry, not a boss rush.
- Why the towns/forts exist at all: the bakeries are built in the
  bones of war — the courtyard concept art's siege-works-turned-
  kitchen read is correct.

## What this binds

- **The semantic audit (plans/15 item 12 / plans/16 slice 5.5):** the
  voice is a dwarf of the after-war bakery. Money strings speak
  realm-pays ("the realm settles the order"), never giant-pays.
  Artillery vocabulary stays exempt flavor — a siege engineer turned
  baker still talks like one.
- **Plans/16 embodiment tone:** arrival/verdict choreography plays a
  guest being welcomed and fed, per the tone rulings above.
- **The naming discussion (plans/16 §6.4)** may draw on this well.

## What this does NOT bind

No story mode, no cutscenes, no lore screens, no mechanics. The lore
is delivered the way party-game lore should be: through strings,
bodies, and what the game conspicuously refuses to do (hurt anyone).
